Why do you need an external backup if you're already in the cloud?

Microsoft 365, Google Workspace and the main cloud providers operate under a shared responsibility model. The provider guarantees that the service is available, but protecting, retaining and recovering your data is your responsibility.

Microsoft / Google guarantee

Cloud provider responsibility

  • Service availability (uptime)
  • Physical security and infrastructure
  • Internal replication between data centres
  • Recycle bin with limited expiry
  • Platform patches and updates

Your business is responsible for

What the provider does NOT cover

  • Protection of your data
  • Identities and access control
  • Configuration and compliance
  • Independent backup and long-term retention
  • Recovery from deletions or ransomware

This means there are critical situations that an independent external backup can solve and the native cloud cannot:

  • Accidental or intentional deletions that exceed recycle bin retention periods.
  • Ransomware attacks that encrypt both productive data and internal copies.
  • Synchronisation errors that replicate deletions to all devices.
  • User account removal after leavers, where associated data is lost.
  • Regulatory compliance requirements (NIS2, ENS, ISO 27001) that demand independent and verifiable copies.

Without a properly configured, monitored and tested external backup, the loss can be permanent.

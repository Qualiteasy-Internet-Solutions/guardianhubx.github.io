The Definition: Beyond the Theory

In simple terms, cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, servers, mobile devices, electronic systems, networks, and data from malicious attacks. It's also known as information technology security or electronic information security.

But in the real world, cybersecurity is digital survival. As our lives and businesses move to the cloud, attackers evolve too. Nowadays, a cyberattack isn't a matter of "if" but "when."

Frequently Asked Questions

Cybersecurity is the practice of protecting computers, servers, mobile devices, electronic systems, networks, and data from malicious attacks. In the real world, it’s the technological and cultural strategy that safeguards what matters most: your information, your operations, and your digital peace of mind.

The CIA Triad comprises the three fundamental pillars of information security: Confidentiality (only authorized parties access data), Integrity (data remains unaltered), and Availability (systems are operational when needed). These principles guide all modern cybersecurity strategies.

Phishing is the most common threat and initiates 91% of successful attacks. It involves deception through fake emails or messages designed to make you reveal confidential information or download malware. It’s the entry point for most targeted attacks.

Ransomware is a type of malware that encrypts your files or systems, blocking access and demanding a ransom to unlock them. It’s especially dangerous because it can completely paralyze a company’s operations within hours. Today it’s present in 44% of all recorded attacks.

Start with the basics: strong and unique passwords, multi-factor authentication on critical accounts, keep software updated, and develop awareness about phishing. If you’re a business, conduct a cybersecurity test to identify specific vulnerabilities. Read our beginner’s guide to apply these measures.

Awareness is the training and education that empowers employees and users to recognize threats and act correctly. The most advanced technology is useless if a user clicks the wrong link. That’s why awareness is the most important component of any modern cybersecurity strategy.

AI is both. On one hand, it enhances defenses by detecting threats in real time. On the other, cybercriminals use AI to create more sophisticated, personalized, and harder-to-detect attacks. 87% of organizations consider AI-related risks to be their fastest-growing cybersecurity threat in 2025.

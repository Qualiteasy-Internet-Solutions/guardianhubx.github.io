<p class="landing-section-subtitle landing-fade-in">The most critical definitions to protect your enterprise in 2026</p>

<h3>Phishing</h3>

</div>

<div class="landing-benefit-card">
<div class="landing-benefit-icon">

<p>Malware that encrypts files and demands payment for decryption. Can paralyze entire organizations within hours.</p>

</div>
<div class="col-lg-6 col-md-6 landing-fade-in">
<div class="landing-benefit-card">
<div class="landing-benefit-icon">
<i class="fas fa-shield"></i>
</div>
<h3>Zero Trust</h3>
<p>Security model that verifies every user and device continuously, regardless of location. No implicit trust, ever.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div class="col-lg-6 col-md-6 landing-fade-in">
<div class="landing-benefit-card">
<div class="landing-benefit-icon">

</div>
<h3>Firewall</h3>
<p>Network security system that monitors and controls incoming/outgoing traffic based on predefined rules.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div class="col-lg-6 col-md-6 landing-fade-in">
<div class="landing-benefit-card">

<i class="fas fa-virus"></i>

<h3>Malware</h3>
<p>Any software designed to harm, exploit, or gain unauthorized access to systems. Umbrella term for viruses, trojans, ransomware, etc.</p>
</div>
</div>
<div class="col-lg-6 col-md-6 landing-fade-in">
<div class="landing-benefit-card">
<div class="landing-benefit-icon">

</div>
</div>

<div class="landing-benefit-icon">

<h3>Advanced Persistent Threat (APT)</h3>

<h3>Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)</h3>
<p>Verification using 2+ methods (password + SMS, biometric, etc.). Dramatically increases account security.</p>

<div class="landing-benefit-icon">

<p>Encrypted tunnel for internet traffic, hiding user identity and location. Protects data from interception on public networks.</p>

</div>
<div class="col-lg-6 col-md-6 landing-fade-in">
<div class="landing-benefit-card">
<div class="landing-benefit-icon">

<div class="landing-benefit-icon">
<i class="fas fa-database"></i>

<h3>Backup</h3>
<p>Complete data copy stored separately. Your final safety net against ransomware, disasters, and data loss.</p>
</div>
</div>

<div class="landing-benefit-card">
<div class="landing-benefit-icon">
<i class="fas fa-bell"></i>

<h3>Incident Response</h3>
<p>Planned procedures to detect, contain, and recover from security breaches. Speed matters—preparation prevents chaos.</p>
</div>

<section class="landing-section landing-section-alt">
<div class="container">
<h2 class="landing-section-title landing-fade-in">Complete A–Z Cybersecurity Glossary</h2>
<p class="landing-section-subtitle landing-fade-in">100+ essential terms for 2026</p>

<div class="mt-5">
<div class="row g-3">
<div class="col-lg-6">
<h4 class="glossary-letter">A</h4>
<ul class="glossary-list">
<li><strong>Account Takeover (ATO)</strong> — Unauthorized access to user accounts, often via phishing or credential stuffing.</li>
<li><strong>Advanced Persistent Threat (APT)</strong> — Long-term, targeted attacks by sophisticated groups seeking persistent network access.</li>
<li><strong>API Security</strong> — Protection of application programming interfaces from unauthorized access and attacks.</li>
<li><strong>Attack Surface</strong> — All possible points where an attacker could exploit your systems and networks.</li>
<li><strong>Authentication</strong> — Process of verifying user identity before granting access to systems or data.</li>
<li><strong>Authorization</strong> — Process of determining what authenticated users are allowed to access.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="col-lg-6">
<h4 class="glossary-letter">B</h4>
<ul class="glossary-list">
<li><strong>Backdoor</strong> — Hidden access point in software that allows unauthorized entry, bypassing normal authentication.</li>
<li><strong>Backup</strong> — Complete copy of data stored separately. Essential protection against ransomware and disasters.</li>
<li><strong>Botnet</strong> — Network of compromised devices controlled by attackers to launch attacks or send spam.</li>
<li><strong>Breach</strong> — Unauthorized access to systems or data, resulting in potential exposure of sensitive information.</li>
<li><strong>Business Continuity</strong> — Plans and procedures to maintain operations during and after security incidents or disasters.</li>
<li><strong>Brute Force Attack</strong> — Method of trying many password combinations until finding the correct one.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="col-lg-6">
<h4 class="glossary-letter">C</h4>
<ul class="glossary-list">
<li><strong>Certificate Authority (CA)</strong> — Organization that issues and verifies digital certificates for secure communication.</li>
<li><strong>Cipher</strong> — Algorithm used to encrypt and decrypt data.</li>
<li><strong>Compliance</strong> — Meeting regulatory requirements (GDPR, HIPAA, ISO 27001, etc.).</li>
<li><strong>Confidentiality</strong> — Ensuring information is only accessible to authorized individuals.</li>
<li><strong>Credential Stuffing</strong> — Attacking accounts using passwords obtained from other breaches.</li>
<li><strong>Cryptography</strong> — Science of securing communication through encryption and codes.</li>
<li><strong>Cyber Attack</strong> — Deliberate attempt to compromise systems, networks, or data.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="col-lg-6">
<h4 class="glossary-letter">D</h4>
<ul class="glossary-list">
<li><strong>Data Exfiltration</strong> — Unauthorized extraction of sensitive data from systems.</li>
<li><strong>DDoS Attack</strong> — Distributed Denial of Service: overwhelming systems with traffic to cause unavailability.</li>
<li><strong>Defense in Depth</strong> — Strategy using multiple layers of security controls to protect systems.</li>
<li><strong>Denial of Service (DoS)</strong> — Attack that prevents legitimate users from accessing systems or services.</li>
<li><strong>Digital Forensics</strong> — Investigation of security incidents through analysis of digital evidence.</li>
<li><strong>Disaster Recovery</strong> — Plans to restore systems and data after catastrophic failures.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="col-lg-6">
<h4 class="glossary-letter">E</h4>
<ul class="glossary-list">
<li><strong>Encryption</strong> — Converting data to code readable only with correct key. Protects data at rest and in transit.</li>
<li><strong>End-to-End Encryption (E2E)</strong> — Only sender and receiver can read messages; provider cannot access content.</li>
<li><strong>Endpoint</strong> — Any device connected to network (computers, phones, servers, IoT devices).</li>
<li><strong>Exploit</strong> — Code or technique used to attack a vulnerability. The bridge between vulnerability and attack.</li>
<li><strong>Exposure</strong> — Unprotected data or system accessible to attackers.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="col-lg-6">
<h4 class="glossary-letter">F</h4>
<ul class="glossary-list">
<li><strong>Firewall</strong> — Network security system controlling incoming/outgoing traffic based on predefined rules.</li>
<li><strong>Forensics</strong> — Investigation and analysis of security incidents using digital evidence.</li>
<li><strong>Framework</strong> — Set of guidelines and best practices (NIST, ISO 27001, CIS) for implementing security.</li>
<li><strong>Full Disk Encryption (FDE)</strong> — Encrypting entire storage device so data is unreadable without correct key.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="col-lg-6">
<h4 class="glossary-letter">G</h4>
<ul class="glossary-list">
<li><strong>Geofencing</strong> — Security control restricting access based on geographic location.</li>
<li><strong>Governance</strong> — Framework of policies and procedures for managing security risks.</li>
<li><strong>Green Team</strong> — Security team defending against Red Team (attackers) in exercises.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="col-lg-6">
<h4 class="glossary-letter">H</h4>
<ul class="glossary-list">
<li><strong>Hacker</strong> — Person who gains unauthorized access to systems. Can be malicious (attacker) or ethical (security researcher).</li>
<li><strong>Hash</strong> — One-way function converting data into fixed-length string. Detects tampering.</li>
<li><strong>HIPAA</strong> — Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act. Regulation for protecting healthcare data.</li>
<li><strong>Host-Based Intrusion Detection (HIDS)</strong> — Software monitoring individual devices for suspicious activity.</li>
<li><strong>Honeypot</strong> — Decoy system designed to attract attackers and log their activities.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="col-lg-6">
<h4 class="glossary-letter">I</h4>
<ul class="glossary-list">
<li><strong>Identification</strong> — Process of claiming an identity in a system (username).</li>
<li><strong>Identity and Access Management (IAM)</strong> — Systems controlling who accesses what resources.</li>
<li><strong>Incident</strong> — Violation of security policy or unwanted security event.</li>
<li><strong>Incident Response</strong> — Planned procedures to detect, contain, and recover from security breaches.</li>
<li><strong>Integrity</strong> — Ensuring data hasn't been altered without authorization.</li>
<li><strong>Intrusion Detection System (IDS)</strong> — Network monitoring system identifying suspicious activity and attacks.</li>
<li><strong>Intrusion Prevention System (IPS)</strong> — System similar to IDS but actively blocks detected attacks.</li>
<li><strong>IoT Security</strong> — Securing Internet of Things devices connected to networks.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="col-lg-6">
<h4 class="glossary-letter">J</h4>
<ul class="glossary-list">
<li><strong>JSON Web Token (JWT)</strong> — Secure token format used for transmitting information between systems.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="col-lg-6">
<h4 class="glossary-letter">K</h4>
<ul class="glossary-list">
<li><strong>Key Exchange</strong> — Process of securely sharing encryption keys between parties.</li>
<li><strong>Key Management</strong> — Processes for storing, distributing, and rotating encryption keys securely.</li>
<li><strong>Keylogger</strong> — Software or hardware capturing keystrokes. Can be used for attacks or legitimate monitoring.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="col-lg-6">
<h4 class="glossary-letter">L</h4>
<ul class="glossary-list">
<li><strong>Lateral Movement</strong> — Attacker moving through network after initial access, accessing additional systems.</li>
<li><strong>Least Privilege</strong> — Principle of giving users/systems only minimum access needed to function.</li>
<li><strong>Log Monitoring</strong> — Analyzing system logs to detect suspicious activities and threats.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="col-lg-6">
<h4 class="glossary-letter">M</h4>
<ul class="glossary-list">
<li><strong>Malware</strong> — Any software designed to harm, exploit, or gain unauthorized access. Umbrella term.</li>
<li><strong>Man-in-the-Middle (MITM)</strong> — Attack intercepting communication between two parties.</li>
<li><strong>Mandatory Access Control (MAC)</strong> — System where administrator controls all access permissions.</li>
<li><strong>Mobile Device Management (MDM)</strong> — Managing security on phones and tablets.</li>
<li><strong>Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)</strong> — Verification using 2+ methods (password + SMS, biometric, etc.).</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="col-lg-6">
<h4 class="glossary-letter">N</h4>
<ul class="glossary-list">
<li><strong>Network Segmentation</strong> — Dividing network into isolated sections to limit breach impact.</li>
<li><strong>Network Security</strong> — Protecting networks from unauthorized access and attacks.</li>
<li><strong>Non-Repudiation</strong> — Preventing someone from denying they performed an action.</li>
<li><strong>NIST Cybersecurity Framework</strong> — Guidelines for managing cybersecurity risks (U.S. standard).</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="col-lg-6">
<h4 class="glossary-letter">O</h4>
<ul class="glossary-list">
<li><strong>OAuth</strong> — Open standard allowing users to authenticate without sharing passwords.</li>
<li><strong>Onion Routing</strong> — Technology (Tor) anonymizing internet traffic through multiple layers.</li>
<li><strong>OSINT (Open Source Intelligence)</strong> — Gathering intelligence from publicly available sources.</li>
<li><strong>Overflow Attack</strong> — Exploiting programs by sending more data than buffer can hold.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="col-lg-6">
<h4 class="glossary-letter">P</h4>
<ul class="glossary-list">
<li><strong>Patch</strong> — Software update fixing vulnerabilities and security issues.</li>
<li><strong>Patch Management</strong> — Process of regularly applying security updates to systems.</li>
<li><strong>Penetration Testing</strong> — Authorized security testing where experts attempt to breach systems.</li>
<li><strong>Phishing</strong> — Social engineering attacks deceiving users into revealing credentials or installing malware.</li>
<li><strong>Privilege Escalation</strong> — Exploiting vulnerabilities to gain higher-level access.</li>
<li><strong>Public Key Infrastructure (PKI)</strong> — System managing digital certificates and encryption keys.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="col-lg-6">
<h4 class="glossary-letter">Q</h4>
<ul class="glossary-list">
<li><strong>Quarantine</strong> — Isolating suspected malicious files or systems to prevent spread.</li>
<li><strong>Quishing</strong> — Phishing attacks using QR codes to direct victims to malicious sites.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="col-lg-6">
<h4 class="glossary-letter">R</h4>
<ul class="glossary-list">
<li><strong>Ransomware</strong> — Malware encrypting files and demanding payment for decryption.</li>
<li><strong>Recovery Point Objective (RPO)</strong> — Maximum acceptable data loss measured in time.</li>
<li><strong>Recovery Time Objective (RTO)</strong> — Maximum acceptable time to restore systems after failure.</li>
<li><strong>Red Team</strong> — Security professionals authorized to attack systems to find weaknesses.</li>
<li><strong>Reverse Engineering</strong> — Analyzing software to understand how it works, often to find vulnerabilities.</li>
<li><strong>Risk Assessment</strong> — Process of identifying and evaluating security risks.</li>
<li><strong>Risk Management</strong> — Strategies to reduce and manage security risks.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="col-lg-6">
<h4 class="glossary-letter">S</h4>
<ul class="glossary-list">
<li><strong>Sandbox</strong> — Isolated environment for safely testing untrusted software.</li>
<li><strong>Secure Shell (SSH)</strong> — Encrypted protocol for secure remote system access.</li>
<li><strong>Security Awareness Training</strong> — Educating employees about security threats and best practices.</li>
<li><strong>Security Information and Event Management (SIEM)</strong> — Platform aggregating and analyzing security logs.</li>
<li><strong>Security Incident</strong> — Breach or violation of security policy.</li>
<li><strong>Smishing</strong> — Phishing attacks via SMS text messages.</li>
<li><strong>Social Engineering</strong> — Manipulating people into revealing confidential information.</li>
<li><strong>Software-as-a-Service (SaaS) Security</strong> — Securing cloud-based applications.</li>
<li><strong>SSL/TLS</strong> — Protocols encrypting data in transit between systems and websites.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="col-lg-6">
<h4 class="glossary-letter">T</h4>
<ul class="glossary-list">
<li><strong>Threat</strong> — Potential cause of security breach (attacker, malware, natural disaster).</li>
<li><strong>Threat Intelligence</strong> — Information about cyber threats used to improve defenses.</li>
<li><strong>Threat Model</strong> — Analysis identifying potential threats to a system.</li>
<li><strong>Token</strong> — Credential proving identity or permission to access resources.</li>
<li><strong>Trojan (Trojan Horse)</strong> — Malware disguised as legitimate software.</li>
<li><strong>Tunnel</strong> — Encrypted connection through network for secure communication.</li>
<li><strong>Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)</strong> — MFA using exactly two verification methods.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="col-lg-6">
<h4 class="glossary-letter">U</h4>
<ul class="glossary-list">
<li><strong>User Access Control (UAC)</strong> — System prompting for permission before allowing privileged actions.</li>
<li><strong>User Behavior Analytics (UBA)</strong> — Technology detecting suspicious user activity patterns.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="col-lg-6">
<h4 class="glossary-letter">V</h4>
<ul class="glossary-list">
<li><strong>Virus</strong> — Malware that replicates itself by attaching to host programs.</li>
<li><strong>Virtual Private Network (VPN)</strong> — Encrypted tunnel hiding user identity and protecting data on public networks.</li>
<li><strong>Vulnerability</strong> — Weakness in software, hardware, or processes exploitable by attackers.</li>
<li><strong>Vulnerability Assessment</strong> — Systematic scanning to identify weaknesses in systems.</li>
<li><strong>Vulnerability Management</strong> — Process of finding, evaluating, and patching vulnerabilities.</li>
<li><strong>Vishing</strong> — Voice phishing: phone calls deceiving victims into revealing information.</li>
</ul>
</div>
<div class="col-lg-6">
<h4 class="glossary-letter">W</h4>
<ul class="glossary-list">

<li><strong>White Hat</strong> — Ethical hacker using skills to improve security (opposite of Black Hat attacker).</li>
<li><strong>Whitelisting</strong> — Allowing only pre-approved software or IP addresses (more secure than blacklisting).</li>
<li><strong>Worm</strong> — Self-replicating malware spreading across networks without requiring user interaction.</li>

<ul class="glossary-list">

<li><strong>Zero Trust Architecture</strong> — Complete implementation of Zero Trust principles across all systems.</li>
